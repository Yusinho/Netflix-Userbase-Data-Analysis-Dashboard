# Netflix-Userbase-Data-Analysis-Dashboard
This Power BI dashboard provides a comprehensive view of Netflix’s user base, revenue performance, and engagement patterns across subscription types, demographics, devices, and geographic regions. The platform records total revenue of $31.27K from a user base of 2,500, with an average revenue per user of $12.5 and a Premium user penetration of 26.32 percent, indicating that while the platform has achieved moderate scale, its monetization potential remains underutilized due to the relatively low proportion of high-value subscribers.

Revenue distribution across subscription tiers shows that the Basic plan contributes the highest share at $12.5K, followed by Standard at $9.6K and Premium at $9.2K. This suggests that a significant portion of users are concentrated in lower-priced plans, limiting overall revenue growth despite a reasonable user base. The low contribution from Premium users, despite higher pricing, points to weak conversion or retention at the top tier, highlighting an opportunity to improve upselling strategies and perceived value.

User demographics reveal that Adults account for 59.64 percent of the total user base, followed by Young Adults at 36.64 percent, while Seniors represent only 3.72 percent. This indicates a strong concentration within the working-age population, with minimal penetration among older users. The skew suggests that content offerings and pricing structures are currently more aligned with the preferences and consumption patterns of younger and middle-aged audiences, leaving a gap in engagement with older demographics.

Device usage is evenly distributed across Laptop, Tablet, Smartphone, and Smart TV, with usage figures ranging closely between 610 and 636. This balance indicates that the platform is well-optimized across multiple devices and that users access content flexibly without reliance on a single dominant channel. It also implies that any performance improvements or user experience enhancements should be applied consistently across all device types to maintain engagement.

Geographic distribution highlights a broad global presence with stronger concentration in regions such as North America and Europe, suggesting that revenue generation is likely driven by more mature markets. This raises questions around growth potential in emerging regions, where user acquisition may exist but monetization levels could be lower due to pricing sensitivity or content localization gaps.

The yearly growth trend shows a sharp increase in revenue to approximately $30,635 in 2022, followed by a steep decline to about $460 in 2023 after a very low base in 2021. This pattern indicates a period of significant expansion or heightened engagement in 2022, possibly driven by external factors or strategic initiatives, but the subsequent drop suggests challenges in sustaining growth, potentially due to user churn, reduced engagement, or market saturation.

Overall, the dashboard indicates that while Netflix maintains a stable and diversified user base with strong accessibility across devices, revenue performance is largely volume-driven rather than value-driven.
